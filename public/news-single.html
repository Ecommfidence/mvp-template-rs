Sherman Could See A Future of Talking Ball

Posted by:
Richard Sherman

It’s not uncommon for star athletes to make his or her way to the broadcast booth or a studio show after retiring, and Richard Sherman hinted during Wednesday’s press conference that he might be interested in a career of talking football when his NFL career comes to an end.

“Yeah I do,” Sherman said. “Talking ball in some way, shape or form. I’m pretty knowledgeable about the game so I’d love to keep talking about it as long as I could, when I’m available and not with my kids.

Tony Romo has made a name for himself as a color commentator on Sundays this season. The former Dallas Cowboys quarterback suffered an injury that sidelined him for the first half of the season. With Dak Prescott’s rise, Romo was ready to step back from the league, and he happily accepted the gig as Jim Nantz’s sidekick.

Romo is one of dozens of former NFL players who have successfully transitioned to a major talking head of the league, and it’s easy to see how Sherman would fit the mold. His knowledge, confidence and stage presence certainly matches the description.

For now, the Seahawks’ corner back looks toward Sunday’s tilt at home against division rival Los Angeles Rams.

Written By: Ben Faunce
